WHC Den Haag

  • Client: WHC Den Haag
  • Team: Individual
  • Year: 2022
  • Deliverables: Prototype

Welcome to my case study on the redesign of the website for WHC Den Haag, a handball club in need of a better online presence. The objective was to improve the usability of the website and add features that would attract new members.

To achieve this, I conducted extensive research and testing to identify pain points and areas for improvement. I then created prototypes based on the findings to present to the stakeholders.

Throughout the process, I worked closely with the team at WHC Den Haag to ensure that the design aligned with their vision and goals. The end result was a sleek and modern website that not only met the needs of the club but also attracted new members.

In this case study, I will outline my approach, the research and testing methods used, and the solutions implemented. Join me as we explore the journey towards a successful website redesign for WHC Den Haag.

Process

I began the process of developing prototypes for WHC Den Haag by conducting extensive research. This included gathering information about the website's current capabilities and the needs of its users. I conducted interviews with users and looked at competitors to gain a better understanding of the market.

Based on this research, I set up a usability test to identify the pain points in the current website and come up with solutions with the client. After conducting the test with actual users, I gathered their feedback and needs for the website.

Using this information, I began designing wireframes according to the user needs. I tested the wireframes with users to ensure they were intuitive and easy to use. Once I was satisfied with the wireframes, I created visual prototypes that showcased the new designs.

After conducting more user tests, I refined the prototypes further and came out with a finished product. This included a visual prototype for the homepage of the website, which was designed to be more user-friendly and intuitive, as well as a completely new page for signing up new members. The entire process was designed to create a website that was both user-focused and functional, improving the usability of the site and attracting more members to the club.

Results

In the redesign of WHC Den Haag's website, I focused on creating a more intuitive and user-friendly experience for both the club's members and potential new members. This involved a complete overhaul of the homepage design, utilizing UX patterns and modern design trends to create a visually appealing and functional layout.

In addition to the homepage, I also added a completely new sign-up page to the site, streamlining the process for new members and making it easier for them to join the club. To ensure that the design and functionality of the site met the needs of both users and the client, I conducted extensive usability testing with actual users.

The final result is a website that not only looks great but also provides a seamless user experience, covering all the necessary user needs identified through the research and testing process. Overall, I am confident that the redesigned website will attract new members to the club and improve the experience of existing members.
